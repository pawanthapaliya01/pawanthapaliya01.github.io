Project 1: Astrocytic Na‚Å∫ Homeostasis and Neuron-Astrocyte Interaction

Project 2: NBCe1 Activity in Astrocytes During Chemical Ischemia

In my second project, I investigated the role of the sodium-bicarbonate cotransporter NBCe1 in astrocytes under brief chemical ischemia. Using organotypic mouse cortical slices, I measured intracellular pH and sodium dynamics with and without NBCe1 inhibition, and in NBCe1 knockout mice. I found that NBCe1 contributes to sodium influx and mitigates intracellular acidification, but also increases ATP consumption under metabolic stress. My experimental observations were supported by a biophysical model that predicted the inward activity of NBCe1 during ischemia and its effects on astrocytic sodium, pH, and energy balance. These results suggest that NBCe1 has both protective (preventing acidosis) and potentially harmful (increasing energy demand) roles in ischemic conditions. [PDF]

Project 3: Neuron- and Astrocyte-Specific NCX Roles During Spreading Depolarizations

The third project focused on the Na‚Å∫/Ca¬≤‚Å∫ exchanger (NCX) in neurons and astrocytes during spreading depolarizations following stroke. I combined experimental data with a computational model to examine how deletion of NCX1 in either cell type affects calcium, sodium, and extracellular glutamate dynamics. I discovered that neuronal NCX reverse-mode activity contributes to detrimental calcium overload and lowers the threshold for spreading depolarizations, while astrocytic NCX reverse-mode activity is protective, reducing extracellular glutamate and calcium influx into neurons. This project highlighted the cell-type-specific roles of NCX and their implications for neuronal survival after stroke, emphasizing the need to consider these differences when targeting NCX therapeutically. In my fourth project, I examined how baseline intracellular sodium and isoform composition of Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫-ATPase shape astrocytic sodium, calcium, and pH dynamics under both physiological and ischemic conditions. Using compartmental modeling and isoform-specific pump kinetics, I found that alpha2beta2 subunits maintain lower sodium and respond sharply to perturbations, whereas alpha2beta1 subunits tolerate higher sodium but are more vulnerable under metabolic stress.
