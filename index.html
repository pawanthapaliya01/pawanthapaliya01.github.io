Projects

Project 1: Astrocytic Na‚Å∫ Homeostasis and Neuron-Astrocyte Interaction

This is the first project of my doctoral thesis. I developed a detailed computational model of astrocytic sodium (Na‚Å∫) and calcium (Ca¬≤‚Å∫) dynamics in cortical and hippocampal astrocytes. The model incorporated experimental data on NMDA and AMPA receptor activity, glutamate transport, and intracellular ion fluxes. It reproduced observed changes in astrocytic sodium under different pharmacological conditions and predicted how Na‚Å∫ homeostasis affects calcium signaling, glutamate uptake, and energy metabolism. One key finding was that cortical astrocytes, due to higher NMDA receptor expression, experience higher ATP consumption under high activity compared to hippocampal astrocytes. [PDF]

Project 2: NBCe1 Activity in Astrocytes During Chemical Ischemia

In this project, I investigated the role of the sodium-bicarbonate cotransporter NBCe1 in astrocytes under brief chemical ischemia. Using organotypic mouse cortical slices, I measured intracellular pH and sodium dynamics with and without NBCe1 inhibition, and in NBCe1 knockout mice. I found that NBCe1 contributes to sodium influx and mitigates intracellular acidification, but also increases ATP consumption under metabolic stress. A biophysical model supported these experimental observations. [PDF]

Project 3: Neuron- and Astrocyte-Specific NCX Roles During Spreading Depolarizations

This project focused on the Na‚Å∫/Ca¬≤‚Å∫ exchanger (NCX) in neurons and astrocytes during spreading depolarizations following stroke. Combining experimental data with a computational model, I examined how deletion of NCX1 in either cell type affects calcium, sodium, and extracellular glutamate dynamics. I discovered that neuronal NCX reverse-mode activity contributes to calcium overload and lowers the threshold for spreading depolarizations, while astrocytic NCX reverse-mode activity is protective, reducing extracellular glutamate and calcium influx into neurons. Project 4: Isoform-Specific Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫-ATPase and Baseline Sodium in Astrocytic Ionic and pH Dynamics

In this project, I examined how baseline intracellular sodium and isoform composition of Na‚Å∫/K‚Å∫-ATPase shape astrocytic sodium, calcium, and pH dynamics under physiological and ischemic conditions. Using compartmental modeling and isoform-specific pump kinetics, I found that alpha2beta2 subunits maintain lower sodium and respond sharply to perturbations, whereas alpha2beta1 subunits tolerate higher sodium but are more vulnerable under metabolic stress. Baseline sodium strongly gated calcium accumulation via NCX and NBCe1, while pH dynamics reflected the balance between NBCe1-driven alkalinization and NHE-driven acidification. [To be submitted]
