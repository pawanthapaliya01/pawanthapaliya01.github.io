Hi, I'm Pawan Thapaliya, a Computational Neuroscientist. Passionate about developing models to explore brain dynamics and solve complex neurological challenges.

About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Applied Physics with a specialization in Computational Neuroscience. My research focuses on understanding the complex interactions between neuronal and non-neuronal cells under metabolic stress conditions, particularly in the context of ischemic events, epilepsy, and neurodegenerative diseases.

Research Interests

  • Computational modeling of astrocyte function during ischemia
  • Neuroinformatics and brain-computer interfaces
  • Machine learning applications in EEG signal analysis
  • Translational research in stroke, epilepsy, and Alzheimer's disease

Current Research

At Dr. Ullah's Biophysics Lab, I'm developing detailed Markov models to simulate ion dynamics in astrocytes, focusing on sodium and calcium signaling during chemical ischemia.

Recent Projects

  • Biophysical modeling of NMDA and AMPA receptor activation
  • Analysis of astrocytic sodium dynamics using single-cell simulations
  • Predictive modeling of ATP consumption in neocortical vs. hippocampal astrocytes

Skills and Expertise

  • Programming and Tools: Python (NumPy, SciPy, TensorFlow, Keras, PyTorch), Fortran, Matlab, Kaggle
  • Computational Techniques: Single-cell modeling, Markov kinetics, Metropolis Hasting, differential equations, digital signal processing
  • Machine Learning: LSTM, SVM, data analysis, feature extraction from EEG signals
  • Computer Vision: Image detection, classification, and segmentation
  • Data Visualization: Matplotlib, Seaborn, Pandas, NeuroPy
